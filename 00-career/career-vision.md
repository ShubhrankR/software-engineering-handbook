# Why do I want to leave VISA?

I don't want to leave VISA because it's a bad company. In fact, I believe VISA is a great company with a healthy culture, good employee benefits, and plenty of opportunities for growth.

My reason for considering a change is personal and professional.

I have spent the last four years in the same role and, over time, I have become too comfortable. My current work no longer challenges me, and I don't feel that I am growing as an engineer. The engineering problems I solve today are not significantly different from those I solved a few years ago.

Another challenge has been my current team environment. Frequent comparisons between teammates have negatively affected my confidence. Instead of feeling encouraged to improve, I often feel hesitant to take on new opportunities.

The rapid advancement of AI has also forced me to reflect on my career. I find myself relying on AI to generate code instead of deeply understanding the underlying concepts. While AI is an incredible productivity tool, I don't want to become dependent on it at the cost of my own engineering skills.

What concerns me the most is that my confidence has steadily declined. I avoid applying for internal roles because I fear being exposed on software engineering fundamentals during interviews. I have built real production systems over the years, yet I still question whether I know enough. This gap between my experience and my confidence has become one of the biggest obstacles in my career.

I don't want to spend the next three years repeating the same pattern.

I want to become the kind of engineer who understands software from first principles, can confidently explain technical decisions, build products end-to-end, embrace AI as a force multiplier instead of a crutch, and pursue opportunities without fear.

This repository is my commitment to rebuilding that confidence through deliberate learning, consistent practice, and continuous improvement.
